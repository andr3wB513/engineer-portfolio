## The problem

On most projects, instrument information lives in a half-dozen places at once: the
P&IDs, a datasheet file per instrument, an instrument index somebody started two
projects ago, and whatever the vendor sent back. Every document is maintained by hand,
so every revision is a chance for the spec sheet, the instrument list, and the P&ID to
quietly disagree with each other.

## What the Mastersheet does

The Mastersheet makes one workbook the single source of truth for every instrument tag
at a plant or on a project. Enter an instrument's data once — tag, service, process
conditions, materials, signal type — and the workbook generates the deliverables from
it:

- **Instrument spec sheets / datasheets**, formatted and ready to issue for vendor
  quoting
- **Instrument lists and indexes**, filtered however the project needs them
- Consistent tag data across every generated document, because there is only one place
  the data lives

## Why it matters

Documents that used to take days of copy-paste-and-check now come from a refresh, and a
revision means changing one row instead of hunting through a folder of files. It's the
same discipline I bring to process design work at Total Aspect Design, packaged into a
tool any project can use.
