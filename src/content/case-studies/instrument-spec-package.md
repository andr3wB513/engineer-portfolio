## Problem

A process unit revamp required roughly forty field instruments &mdash; flow, level,
pressure, and temperature transmitters, along with several control valves &mdash; to be
specified and competitively bid across multiple vendors on a fixed schedule.

## Approach

- Built instrument datasheets from the P&ID and process data sheets, capturing process
  conditions, materials of construction, hazardous area classification, and required
  accuracy/turndown for each tag.
- Standardized datasheet formatting so instruments could be grouped by type into vendor
  packages, reducing the number of separate inquiries needed.
- Issued the package for competitive quote, then supported technical bid evaluation:
  checking vendor exceptions against the specification and flagging deviations that
  affected process suitability or code compliance.
- Coordinated with I&C engineering to confirm signal types, wiring requirements, and
  power availability were captured before issuing final purchase requisitions.

## Outcome

The package went out to bid on schedule, and the technical bid evaluation surfaced two
vendor exceptions early enough to be resolved before award &mdash; avoiding rework after
purchase.
